“Our greatest weakness lies in giving up. The most certain way to succeed is always to try just one more time” -Thomas A. Edison. Since my early childhood I have been moved by his philosophy: consistency and resilliency. His words encourage me to challenge my comfort zone with the mindset of learning from failures, most importantly enjoying the journey.

I am from Kolkata, an easteren city in India. The city is widely regarded as the “Cultural Capital” of India, and is also nicknamed the “City of Joy” (definitely worth visiting!).

From the very beginning of my school life I realized that my interest is on Mathematics, not merely in solving harder problems, rather in trying to solve them in uncommon but easier way. During school I went through ‘Play With Graphs’ by Arihant, which has unique examples (such as, very common problem of algebra is solved in very unique way using some graphs) which triggered me to think out of the box.

From my school life I was determined to embark my educational journey centering Mathematics and also to have the essence of its practicability in real life; these two motivated me to take Statistics as major in my under-graduation. I got admission in Department of Statistics, St. Xavier’s College, Kolkata. I kept Mathematics and Economics as minors. In my first introduction with statistics, I was very much amazed at how the subject has evolved based on basic logics and common sense. This time I went through the book ‘How to Lie with Statistics’ by Darrell Huff, which gave a very clear picture about how data could easily be misinterpreted; as a fresher I got a different taste of the subject. I was greatly motivated by one of our Economics professor, Mallinath Mukherjee. He used to integrate Economics, Statistics, Politics, Human Psycology and explained how all of these factors influence each other. Instead of providing traditional notes, he used to motivate us to investigate the probable flow of Economics. His unique style of teaching motivated me to constanlty relate all the theories I was learning to the real world. I completed my Masters from University of Calcutta. Those two years in masters are very much important in shaping my interest in the subject. From the very first day of my Masters I felt gratitude for getting the opportunity to be in touch of eminent professors, such as Dr. Sugata Sen Roy, Dr. Bhaswati Ganguli, Dr. Kalyan Das, Dr. Rahul Bhattacharya, Dr. Asis K. Chattopadhyay.
